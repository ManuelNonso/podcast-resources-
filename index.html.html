01 Part One

Surface What Has Been Running on Repeat

You cannot change what you cannot see. Part One makes the invisible visible — the specific memory that has been playing, the story it has been writing about you, and the warm moment that has been starved of your attention in the same season. Do not rush this. The precision of your answers here is everything.

🔊
Exercise 1.1
Name Your Horn Moment
Allow 10–15 minutes

The horn moment is the specific incident — not a vague feeling, not a season, not a category of events — but one precise moment your brain has been returning to. The one that surfaces when things feel uncertain, when you are about to step up, when doubt walks in. Name it here with full honesty. No one else reads this.

The Incident — Describe It Specifically Be precise. Vague descriptions produce vague results. The more specific, the more workable.
What Your Brain Did With It Immediately After

The mind is not replaying this incident because it is the most important thing that happened. It is replaying it because it registered as a threat — to your readiness, your belonging, your capability. Threat gets more airtime than safety. Automatically. Unless you intervene deliberately.

Exercise 1.2
Find Your Bus Driver Moment
Allow 10–15 minutes

The bus driver moment is a positive experience from the same season as your horn moment — real, warm, and almost certainly being ignored right now. It does not need to be dramatic. It needs to be genuine. It is the kind word, the unexpected encouragement, the quiet win that got crowded out by the louder, more persistent horn. It exists. Find it.

Where to look: Think about the same period — the same month, the same chapter, even the same day. Who showed up for you? What went right that you barely noticed? What evidence exists, in that same season, that directly contradicts the verdict your horn moment has been delivering?
The Moment — Describe It in Vivid Detail The more sensory detail you can recall, the more powerful the superimposition practice will be. Do not summarise — describe.

Exercise 1.3
The Evidence Inventory — Full Picture
Allow 10 minutes

Your horn moment has been acting as if it were the only evidence. It is not. This exercise forces a full accounting — because when you see the complete picture, the horn's dominance begins to loosen immediately.

Evidence That Contradicts the Horn's Verdict

List at least five specific examples from your own life — moments, outcomes, feedback, relationships — that directly contradict what the horn has been telling you about yourself.

You do not need to be the world's leading expert. You just need to be one step ahead of the people you want to help — and the evidence of that step exists in your own history. The horn wants you to forget it. This list is how you remember.

02 Part Two

Build Your Superimposition Practice

Knowing what to do is not the same as being able to do it under pressure. Part Two builds the practice — your personalised four-step method, made precise and portable, so that when the horn surfaces (and it will), you are not starting from scratch. You are executing something you have already prepared.

🔁
Exercise 2.1

Allow 15 minutes

The Superimposition Method works because it is specific. Fill in each step using the material you surfaced in Part One. Do not be general. The more personalised and precise this protocol, the more effective it becomes under pressure.

Step 1 — My Horn Moment (Named and Bounded) This is the trigger you are training yourself to intercept.
Step 2 — My Bus Driver Moment (Made Vivid) Read this back to yourself right now. Does it produce the warmth and groundedness of the original moment? If not, add more sensory detail until it does.
Step 3 — My Replacement Trigger
Step 4 — My Repetition Commitment
Important distinction: This is not suppression. You are not pretending the horn moment did not happen. You remember it clearly. You are simply choosing, deliberately and repeatedly, not to give it the only seat at the table. The memory does not disappear — it loses the emotional charge that has been making it feel like a verdict.

Exercise 2.2
The 21-Day Charge Tracker
Ongoing — update daily

The emotional charge of your horn moment does not disappear overnight. It fades through consistent, deliberate practice. This tracker makes that fading visible — which itself accelerates the process. Rate both the horn and the bus driver daily on a scale of 1–10 for emotional intensity. Watch what happens over 21 days.

How to rate: For the horn — 10 means the memory dominates and produces strong self-doubt or anxiety. 1 means it barely registers. For the bus driver — 10 means the memory is vivid, warm, and easily accessible. 1 means it feels distant and flat. The goal: horn goes down, bus driver goes up.

03 Part Three

Hold It When Things Get Hard

The Superimposition Method is easy when life is smooth. The test is whether it holds when the pressure increases — when stress is high, when the horn is very loud, when the temptation to collapse back into the familiar narrative is strongest. These three scenarios test your practice against real conditions.

1
The Relapse — The Horn Comes Back Stronger Than Before
The situation: It is Day 11. The method has been working. Then something happens — a new setback, a difficult conversation, a rejection — and the original horn moment comes roaring back, louder than ever. You feel like you are back at Day 1. The temptation is to interpret this as proof that the method does not work, or that you are somehow failing.
Why this is not failure — write your own answer
Your specific response protocol for a relapse moment

A relapse does not mean the process is not working. It means the process is still needed. The goal was never permanent immunity from the horn. The goal is the ability to intercept it faster, more reliably, with less drama each time it surfaces. That muscle gets stronger through exactly this kind of moment.

04 Part Four

Commit, Track, and Live In Full Expression

The practice works through repetition. Protection means building the conditions under which the repetition actually happens — a clear commitment, a regular audit, an accountability structure, and a final reflection that anchors the transformation. This is not a nice ending. It is the structure that makes the work stick.

🔍
Exercise 4.1
Weekly Audit — Your Five Questions
15 minutes every Sunday evening

Set a recurring Sunday evening reminder. These five questions take fifteen minutes and tell you everything you need to know about how the practice is holding, where it needs attention, and what to prioritise in the coming week. Answer them honestly — not performatively.

  • 1. How many times did the horn surface this week? — Not an estimate. An honest accounting. And how many times did you successfully deploy the replacement?
  • 2. What is the current charge of the horn moment? — Using the 1–10 scale from your tracker. Has it moved since last week? What do the numbers show?
  • 3. How vivid and accessible is the bus driver moment right now? — Can you call it up in seconds? Does it still produce warmth? If not, what has faded and why?
  • 4. Which version of yourself showed up to the most important moment this week? — The one fed by the horn, or the one fed by the smile? What evidence supports your answer?
  • 5. What is one specific adjustment to make this week? — Based on your honest answers to the four questions above, what single change would most improve the practice in the next seven days?
